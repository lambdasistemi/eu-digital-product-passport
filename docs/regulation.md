# Regulatory Framework

## Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR)

**Regulation (EU) 2024/1781** — adopted 13 June 2024, published in the Official Journal on 28 June 2024. Entered into force on 18 July 2024.

The ESPR replaces and extends the old Ecodesign Directive 2009/125/EC (which only covered energy-related products) to cover virtually all physical products placed on the EU market, with exceptions for food, feed, and medicinal products.

### Key articles on DPP

| Article | Topic |
|---------|-------|
| Art. 9 | Digital Product Passport — general requirements |
| Art. 10 | Data carriers (QR, RFID, NFC) |
| Art. 11 | Unique product identifiers |
| Art. 12 | DPP registry |
| Art. 13 | Technical design of the DPP |
| Art. 14 | Access rights and data governance |
| Art. 15 | Customs and market surveillance access |

### Product scope

The ESPR empowers the European Commission to adopt **delegated acts** specifying DPP requirements for specific product categories. Priority product groups identified include:

1. **Iron and steel** — first delegated act expected
2. **Aluminium**
3. **Textiles (apparel and footwear)**
4. **Furniture**
5. **Tyres**
6. **Detergents**
7. **Paints**
8. **Lubricants**
9. **Chemicals**
10. **Electronics and ICT**
11. **Energy-related products** (carried over from old Ecodesign Directive)

## Battery Regulation

**Regulation (EU) 2023/1542** — adopted 12 July 2023, entered into force 17 August 2023. This is the first EU regulation to mandate a Digital Product Passport for a specific product category.

### Battery passport requirements

Applies to:

- **EV batteries** (electric vehicle)
- **Industrial batteries** > 2 kWh
- **LMT batteries** (light means of transport — e-bikes, scooters)

Does NOT apply to portable batteries (AA, AAA, coin cells, etc.) — these have simpler labeling requirements.

### Mandatory data fields (Art. 77)

The battery passport must include:

- Manufacturer identity and contact details
- Battery model and chemistry
- Manufacturing date and place
- Carbon footprint (per kWh) with performance class
- Recycled content percentages (cobalt, lithium, nickel, lead)
- Material composition and hazardous substances
- Rated capacity, voltage, expected lifetime
- State of Health (SoH) tracking over time
- Repurposing and recycling information
- Supply chain due diligence report
- CE marking and conformity declarations

### Battery passport timeline

| Date | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| 18 Feb 2025 | Carbon footprint declaration required |
| 18 Aug 2025 | Carbon footprint performance classes |
| 18 Feb 2027 | **Full battery passport mandatory** for EV and industrial batteries > 2 kWh |
| 18 Aug 2028 | Minimum recycled content thresholds enforced |
| 31 Dec 2030 | Higher recycled content targets |

## Relationship to other EU regulations

The DPP sits within a broader regulatory ecosystem:

- **Green Claims Directive** — companies making environmental claims must substantiate them; DPP data can serve as evidence
- **Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)** — DPP carbon footprint data aligns with CBAM embedded emissions reporting
- **EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR)** — supply chain due diligence requirements overlap with DPP traceability
- **Waste Framework Directive (revision)** — DPP facilitates end-of-life sorting and recycling
- **Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)** — DPP product-level data feeds into company-level sustainability reporting
- **Right to Repair Directive** — DPP provides repair manuals, spare parts info, repairability scores
