    How delegated acts work¶

    The European Commission adopts delegated acts on a rolling basis under ESPR Art. 9. Each delegated act specifies:

    • Which product category is covered
    • What data fields are required in the DPP
    • Whether the DPP is at model, batch, or item level (Art. 9(2)(d))
    • Transition periods for compliance (typically 18-24 months)
    • Verification and auditing requirements

    Once published in the Official Journal, economic operators have the specified transition period to implement the DPP.

    Dates are indicative

    Delegated acts are adopted by the Commission and subject to Parliament/Council scrutiny. Exact dates shift frequently. The ESPR Working Plan 2025-2030 provides the official roadmap. Check EUR-Lex for current status.

    Interacting regulations (no standalone DPP, but linked)¶

    These regulations do not mandate their own DPP but rely on or interact with DPP data:

    Regulation Interaction with DPP
    CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism) DPP carbon footprint data supports CBAM compliance for cement, iron/steel, aluminium, fertilizers, electricity, hydrogen
    CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) Product-level DPP data feeds company-level sustainability reports
    EUDR (EU Deforestation Regulation) Supply chain traceability overlaps for rubber, wood, palm oil products
    Right to Repair Directive DPP provides repair manuals, spare parts info, repairability scores. Transposition deadline: 31 Jul 2026
    Empowering Consumers Directive Bans vague green claims; DPP data substantiates environmental claims
    CSDDD (Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence) DPP carries product-level evidence of supply chain due diligence
    Waste Framework Directive (revision) DPP data facilitates sorting, recycling, and EPR eco-modulation
